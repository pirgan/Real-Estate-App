# Landlord Guide — Meridian Realty Group

## Becoming a Landlord

Investing in property can provide reliable income and long-term capital growth. Meridian's lettings team manages over 800 properties across London, achieving average voids of less than 12 days between tenancies.

## Legal Obligations

Before letting your property you must:
1. **EPC** — minimum rating E. Properties rated F or G cannot be legally let.
2. **Gas Safety Certificate** — annual inspection by a Gas Safe registered engineer.
3. **Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)** — every 5 years.
4. **Smoke and carbon monoxide alarms** — fitted on every floor (smoke) and in rooms with solid-fuel appliances (CO).
5. **Right to Rent checks** — verify tenants' legal right to reside in the UK.
6. **Deposit protection** — place deposit in a government-approved scheme (DPS, MyDeposits, or TDS) within 30 days.
7. **How to Rent guide** — provide current version to tenants on move-in.

## Licensing

Some London boroughs require an HMO (House in Multiple Occupation) licence for properties let to 5+ people from 2+ households. Selective licensing schemes also exist in parts of Newham, Waltham Forest, and Southwark. Meridian checks current requirements for your postcode.

## Rental Yields

**Gross yield** = (Annual rent / Property value) × 100

London gross yields typically range from 3.5% (prime central) to 6% (outer zones). Net yield after costs (management, maintenance, void periods, mortgage interest) averages 2–4%.

## Tax

- Rental income is taxable as part of your income. Allowable deductions include mortgage interest (restricted to basic-rate relief for residential landlords), agent fees, maintenance, insurance, and HMRC-approved capital allowances.
- Capital Gains Tax applies on sale. Annual CGT allowance applies. Higher/additional rate taxpayers pay 24% on residential gains.
- Speak to a specialist property accountant — Meridian can refer you.
