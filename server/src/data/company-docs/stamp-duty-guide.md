# Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) Guide — Meridian Realty Group

## What is Stamp Duty?

Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) is a tax paid by buyers of residential and commercial property in England and Northern Ireland. Scotland uses Land and Buildings Transaction Tax (LBTT) and Wales uses Land Transaction Tax (LTT) — different rules apply.

## Residential SDLT Rates (England, from October 2024)

| Property Value Band | Standard Rate | First-Time Buyer Rate |
|--------------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Up to £250,000 | 0% | 0% |
| £250,001 – £425,000 | 5% | 0% |
| £425,001 – £625,000 | 5% | 5% |
| £625,001 – £925,000 | 5% | 5% |
| £925,001 – £1.5M | 10% | 10% |
| Over £1.5M | 12% | 12% |

*First-time buyer relief is not available on properties over £625,000.*

## Additional Dwelling Surcharge

If you are purchasing an additional residential property (buy-to-let, second home), a 3% surcharge applies to each band above. This applies even if you are replacing your main residence and have not yet sold your previous home (though a refund is available if sold within 3 years).

## Example Calculations

**First-time buyer purchasing £450,000 flat:**
- £0 – £425,000: 0% = £0
- £425,001 – £450,000: 5% of £25,000 = £1,250
- **Total SDLT: £1,250**

**Standard buyer purchasing £750,000 house:**
- £0 – £250,000: 0% = £0
- £250,001 – £750,000: 5% of £500,000 = £25,000
- **Total SDLT: £25,000**

**Buy-to-let investor purchasing £400,000 flat (3% surcharge):**
- £0 – £250,000: 3% of £250,000 = £7,500
- £250,001 – £400,000: 8% of £150,000 = £12,000
- **Total SDLT: £19,500**

## When is SDLT Due?

SDLT must be paid within 14 days of completion. Your solicitor calculates and submits the return on your behalf. Payment is made from the funds received on completion day.

## Non-UK Residents

Non-UK residents purchasing residential property in England pay an additional 2% SDLT surcharge on top of standard rates. This applies to both individuals and companies.

## SDLT on Commercial Property

Commercial rates differ from residential rates. Mixed-use properties (e.g. flat above a shop) may qualify for lower commercial rates — always obtain specialist advice.
