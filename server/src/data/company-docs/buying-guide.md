# Buying a Home with Meridian Realty Group

## Introduction

Purchasing a property is one of the most significant financial decisions you will make. At Meridian Realty Group, our experienced agents guide you through every step of the journey — from initial search to handing over the keys.

## Step 1 — Establish Your Budget

Before viewing any properties, determine your maximum budget. Factor in:
- **Deposit**: Typically 5–20% of the purchase price. A higher deposit secures a better mortgage rate.
- **Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)**: Applies in England and Northern Ireland. First-time buyers are exempt on the first £425,000.
- **Legal fees**: Expect £1,000–£2,500 for a solicitor or licensed conveyancer.
- **Survey costs**: A HomeBuyer Report costs £400–£1,000 depending on property value.
- **Removal costs**: Budget £500–£2,000 depending on distance and volume.

## Step 2 — Get a Mortgage Agreement in Principle

An Agreement in Principle (AIP) from a lender confirms how much you can borrow. It strengthens your offer and signals seriousness to sellers. Speak to an independent mortgage broker — they search the whole market and are often free to buyers.

## Step 3 — Property Search

Use the Meridian portal to filter by location, price, property type, bedrooms, and more. Save favourites and set up instant alerts for new listings matching your criteria.

## Step 4 — Viewings

Our agents arrange accompanied viewings at times that suit you, including evenings and weekends. We recommend visiting a property at least twice — once during the day, once in the evening — to assess natural light, noise, and neighbourhood feel.

## Step 5 — Making an Offer

When you are ready, your Meridian agent submits your offer to the seller. We advise on realistic offer levels based on recent comparable sales. Offers are typically made at or slightly below asking price in a normal market.

## Step 6 — Conveyancing

Once an offer is accepted, instruct your solicitor to begin conveyancing. Key stages include:
1. Draft contract issued by seller's solicitor
2. Property searches (local authority, water, environmental)
3. Mortgage valuation and survey
4. Exchange of contracts (legally binding, 10% deposit paid)
5. Completion (balance paid, keys collected)

## Step 7 — Moving In

On completion day, funds transfer by midday and your agent releases keys. Welcome home!

## First-Time Buyer Schemes

- **Help to Buy (Equity Loan)**: Government lends up to 20% (40% in London) interest-free for five years on new-build homes.
- **Shared Ownership**: Buy a share (10–75%) and pay rent on the remainder.
- **Lifetime ISA**: Save up to £4,000/year and receive a 25% government bonus (max £1,000/year).
