# London Area Guides — Meridian Realty Group

## Kensington & Chelsea

One of London's most prestigious boroughs, Kensington and Chelsea offers world-class museums, fine dining, and iconic garden squares. Property prices average £1.2M–£3M+ for houses and £600K–£1.5M for flats. The area attracts international buyers, professionals, and families seeking top-rated schools including Holland Park School and Chelsea Academy.

**Transport**: District and Circle lines (Kensington High Street, Sloane Square). Excellent bus network.
**Schools**: Outstanding Ofsted ratings across most state and independent schools.
**Lifestyle**: Portobello Road Market, Natural History Museum, Hyde Park, King's Road boutiques.

## Notting Hill

Famous for its colourful townhouses, the annual Carnival, and Portobello Road, Notting Hill is one of London's most desirable addresses. Average house prices: £1.5M–£4M. Garden flats and mews houses command premiums.

**Transport**: Central line (Notting Hill Gate), District and Circle lines.
**Vibe**: Bohemian, multicultural, village-like feel within Zone 1/2.

## Shoreditch & Hackney

The creative heartland of East London. Former industrial spaces converted to loft apartments, proximity to tech companies in Silicon Roundabout, and a thriving restaurant scene. Average flat prices: £450K–£850K.

**Transport**: Overground (Shoreditch High Street, Hoxton), Central line (Liverpool Street 10 min walk).
**Buyers**: Young professionals, creatives, tech workers.

## Walthamstow

One of London's fastest-appreciating areas. Village centre, Lloyd Park, and William Morris Gallery make it uniquely attractive. Average house prices: £450K–£700K — 40% below Hackney.

**Transport**: Victoria line (Walthamstow Central) — 15 minutes to Oxford Circus.
**Schools**: Several Outstanding-rated primaries and secondaries.
**Why buy now**: Strong rental demand, significant new development pipeline.

## Richmond & Twickenham

South-west London at its best. The Thames, Richmond Park, and Kew Gardens make this area exceptional for families. Average house prices: £700K–£1.8M.

**Transport**: South Western Railway to Waterloo (18–30 minutes). District line to Richmond.
**Schools**: Outstanding state schools and proximity to Hampton School and Lady Eleanor Holles.

## Stratford & Olympic Park

Transformed by the 2012 Olympics, Stratford offers modern apartments, Westfield shopping, and Crossrail connections. Average flat prices: £380K–£600K — strong rental yields of 4.5–5.5%.

**Transport**: Elizabeth line (Crossrail) — 7 minutes to Canary Wharf, 15 minutes to Liverpool Street.
**Investors**: Best-value Zone 2/3 area with continued regeneration upside.

## Hampstead

A leafy village atop North London's highest hill. Hampstead Heath, independent boutiques, and period houses define this unique enclave. Average house prices: £1.5M–£5M+.

**Transport**: Northern line (Hampstead). Slow but scenic — many residents drive or cycle.
**Buyers**: Established professionals, international families, academics (proximity to UCL).
